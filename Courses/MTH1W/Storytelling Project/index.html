    Storytelling Project¶

    Quote

    “It is impossible to be a mathematician without being a poet in soul.” - Sofia Kovalevskaya

    Description¶

    Select one topic from the Topic List below or create your own topic. It is always best to select a topic that sounds interesting to you. There are no "difficult" topics. Each student is required to select a unique topic so that no two students have the same topic. Sign up for a topic with your teacher.

    You will research a number concept, an algebraic concept, a geometric concept, or a measurement system to tell a story about its development and use in a specific culture or community, and describe its relevance by making connections to a career, school subject, or our daily experiences.

    To see examples of this project, click here.

    Success Criteria¶

    To meet the requirements of this project you will include all of the following within a 5-minute presentation. The date of your presentation will be decided in class.

    1. A visual component to your story that can be digitally recorded and shared.

    Example

    Digital Creations: Posters, slideshows, recorded videos, audio clips, websites, multimedia, ... Physical Creations: Scrapbook, vision board, collage, paper maches, 3D prints, ...

    1. One part of your story will explain or show a deep connection to a specific culture or community in the present or past. Your deep connection should help others learn about the people of the culture or community in your topic.
    2. One part of your story will explain or show a deep connection to a school subject, a career, or a daily experience in which you are interested.
    3. One part of your story will explain or show a math problem, understandable by Grade 9s, related to your topic.
    4. A Bibliography in MLA or APA Formatting
      1. Use https://zbib.org/, or
      2. NoodleTools (UGDSB)
      3. You need three sources.

    Note

    Wikipedia is often a good resource to start your research but should not be your only source of information.

    A level 0, 1, or 2 represents achievement that has not effectively met one or more of the success criteria outlined as required by this project. An effective presentation creates has clearly defined all 5 requirements through the use of visuals and audio. Practicing your presentation skills is necessary for this project.

    Topic List¶

    You may select a topic from this list or create your own topic. Each description is meant to help you begin your research; any questions asked in this section do not represent the exact requirements of your project (see #Success Criteria)

    Number Concepts¶

    Topic Description
    Zero - What is its history? Why was there a need for this number?
    - Cultures to research: Maya and/or Indian Civilizations
    Infinity and Dividing by Zero - What does infinity mean? Why can we not divide by zero? What is the connection between infinity and 0? What is this concept’s history?
    - Helpful names: Brahmagupta, India; Bhaskara, India
    Negative Numbers - What is the history? How were they thought about?
    - Helpful connections: Brahmagupta, India; China.
    Irrational Numbers - What are they? What is their history? How were they thought about?
    - Helpful connections: YBC 7289, Babylonian
    Pi - What is its history? How did it originate? How does it work? What do we know about it?
    - Helpful connections: Zu Chongzhi, China; Aryabhata, India; Bhaskara, India; Ramanujan, India
    Square Root - What is its history? How does it work? How did people find square roots without calculators?
    - The Arabic word jidr means "plant-root"
    Counting in Zulu - How are fingers used to count? How are larger numbers formed?
    - How does it compare to our counting?
    Origin of names of numbers - Where did “odd” names of numbers come from? (e.g., eleven, twelve)
    Fibonacci Numbers - What are they? How do they work? What is their history? Why are they important?
    The Golden Ratio - What is it? How does it work? Where do we see it in the world? In nature? What is its history?
    Divisibility Rule by 7 - What is it? (Check out Chika Ofili, 12-year-old boy from Nigeria)
    The Ishango Bone - What is it? What mathematical idea(s) connect to it? Where did it from? (Uganda, DRC)
    Magic Squares - What are they? What are they used for? Where did they come from?
    - Helpful names: I Ching, Lo Shu, China
    Abacus - What is it? How does it work? Where does it come from? How is it used today?
    - A.D. 900–1000; Mesoamerican cultures
    Exponential Growth - What is it? When was it first used? Who is the original person (people) to think about it?
    - Helpful Names: Grand Vizier Sissan Ben Dahir, inventor of chess, India
    Pascal’s Triangle - What is it? (how are sums used to create it and how is it related to exponents) Where did it come from? What is its history?
    - Khayyam Triangle, Persia; Blaise Pascal
    The Chinese Remainder Theorem -
    Prime Numbers - The building blocks of all numbers; prime vs composite numbers.
    - Mersenne Primes. What are primes used for?
    Perfect Numbers - What are Perfect Numbers? How can we calculate them?
    - Are they used for any purpose?
    Public Key Cryptography - What is RSA Encryption? Where is Cryptograph used? Who invented the idea?
    Triangular Numbers - What are they? Why are they called triangular?
    Human Computers - Hidden Figures (Movie)
    - How did humans land on the moon?
    The Enigma Machine - A device used in World War II by Nazi Germany to encode secret messages to send them over long distances. Who cracked this code?
    Probability Theory - What are probabilities used for? What is Bayesian Probability? What are the connections of Probabilities to games and gambling? How do Casinos make money?
    Statistics - How does collection of Data affect our lives today? What is done with the Data collected from us?
    - How are statistics used in Science to discover new truths?

    Algebra Concepts¶

    Topic Description
    Variables - When was the first variable used in math? What is the History of use of variables?
    - Use of colour for variables (eg. Brahmagupta)
    Algebra, or
    Hindu or Babylonian Algebra    		 - What is it? What culture / person is credited with starting the use of algebra?
    - Focus on the history over time (e.g., al-jabr, India; Bhaskara's Lilavati, India)
    Cubic Equations - How can cubic expressions be created to represent volume of shapes?
    - Some history: Wang Xiaotong, China; Omar Khayyam, Arabia
    Systems of Equations - What are they? What kinds of problems do they solve?
    - What cultures/people are credited with using them first? What kinds of problems caused their “discovery”?
    - Sample sources:  (Nine Chapters on Mathematical Arts, China; Mahavira, India)
    Matrices - What are they? How are they used?
    - When were they first used? By whom? What is their history? (e.g., China)
    Gaussian Elimination - What is it? How is it used? How does it connect to matrices?
    - What kind of problem(s) does this solve? What culture/person is credited with their use/design in history? (e.g.,  Nine Chapters on Mathematical Arts, China; Mahavira, India)
    Symmetry - Symmetry of graphs (and other representations) of relationships with a focus on linear vs non-linear graphs
    - What is its history? (e.g., Arapaho Symbols, Navajo; Julirde, West Africa)
    Algorithm - What is the history of this word? How does this connect with mathematics? What culture/person is credited with coining this term? (e.g., al-Khowarizmi, Arabia)
    - What did it mean then? What does it mean now?
    Computer Programming - What is the history of computer programming? Who is/are the first person/people to program? (e.g.,  Ada Lovelace, England) 
    - What did it look like? What kinds of problems were solved by the first computer programs?
    Programming Languages (Pick One) - When was Python invented?
    - What are some concepts in the Python language that use math ideas we have explored.
    Arithmetic Sequence & Series - What are they? How do they work? How do they connect with our course?
    - What culture/person is credited with their first recording/discovery? (e.g., China)
    Distributive Property and Factoring - Where was it first used? What is factoring? How do factoring and the distributive property compare?
    Rhind Papyrus - What is it and what is its importance?
    An Interesting Mathematician Examples: Hypatia, Muhammad ibn Musa al-Khwarizmi, Niels Henrik Abel, Srinivasa Ramanujan, Paul Erdős, Maryam Mirzakhani, Terence Tao. Artur Avila, Maryna Viazovska
    The Math Olympiad - Who gets to participate? How do they compete?
    The Fields Medal - How does one win this medal? Who has won it in the past? What do the winners do in the world of mathematics?

    Geometry Concepts¶

    Topic Description
    Geometry - Who is credited with this idea/process? (e.g., Mesoamerican cultures)
    - In what situations was geometry used in history?
    Symmetry - Focus on shapes instead of graphs
    - What is its history? How was it used? (e.g.,  Arapaho Symbols, Navajo; Julirde, West Africa)
    Quadrilaterals - What is this idea’s history? (e.g., Brahmagupta, India)
    - What is special about quadrilaterals? What are some features of special quadrilaterals (e.g., kites, parallelograms, rhombuses, etc.)
    Quilting and Geometry - Learn about quilting in African American, Appalachian, Native American, Pacific Islander, Celtic, and Asian traditions.
    - How are geometric properties used?
    Tessellations - What are they? How do they work?
    - What is their history? How are they used today? (e.g., Islamic mosaics)
    Spherical Triangles - What are they? What is their history? Who is credited with their discovery? (e.g., Guo Shoujing, China)
    Fractals - What are they? Where do we see them in nature?
    - How do they work? Who is credited with first noticing them / naming them / discovering them?
    Mobius Strips - What are they? How do they work? Where did they come from? Why are they important?
    The Platonic Solids - There are only 5 of them - why?
    Side Lengths of Right Triangle Relationship - Where did this idea come from before Pythagoras? What was it used for? Why was it important? (e.g., Nine Chapters on Mathematical Arts, China)
    Side Lengths of Right Triangle Relationship: Pythagorean Triples - What are they? How are they used? Who is credited with finding them? How do they work? (e.g., Plimpton 322, Iraq)
    Girih - Decorative Islamic tilings used in building
    Compass and Straight-Edge Constructions - Used by Euclid in his textbook “The Elements”

    Measurement Systems¶

    Topic Description
    Metric System - Where did it come from? What is its history?
    Imperial System - Where did it come from? What is its history?
    Angles & Degrees - What culture/person is credited with the origins of these measurements? (e.g., Kushyar Ibn Labban, Persia)
    - How do the ideas work?
    Angles & Radians - What are radians and why are they used instead of degrees?
    Angles & Gradians - Where are Gradians used?
    Binary Numbers (Base 2) - What are they? What is their history? (e.g., Pa-Kua, Korea)
    Base 10 - What is its history? (e.g., the Mohenjo Dan, culture of the Indus Valley)
    Base 20 - How does this system work? What is its history? Where is/was it used?  (e.g., Mayan number system, connection to French numbers)
    Base 60 - How does this system work? What is its history? Where is/was it used?  (e.g., Babylonian number system)
    360 Degrees in a Circle - What is its history? Who is credited with this idea? (e.g., Babylonians)
    - Why 360? How was this idea used?
    Hands (to measure horses) - Where does this strange measurement come from?
